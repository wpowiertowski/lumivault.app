External Volumes

LumiVault copies archived photos to external drives for redundant offline storage. Each volume receives a complete mirror of your exported files.

Adding a Volume

  1. Connect an external drive to your Mac.
  2. Open Settings > Volumes (⌘,).
  3. Click Add Volume and select the drive in the file picker.
  4. LumiVault creates a security-scoped bookmark so it can access the volume in future sessions without re-prompting.

Volume Structure

On each volume, LumiVault creates a LumiVault/ directory with this structure:

LumiVault/
  2026/
    04/
      06/
        My Album/
          IMG_1234.jpg
          IMG_1234.jpg.par2
          IMG_1234.jpg.vol0+1.par2
  catalog.json

The date-based folder hierarchy matches the export date you set during import.

Removing a Volume

In Settings > Volumes, click the remove button next to a volume. This only removes the volume from LumiVault's tracking — files on the drive are not deleted.

Volume Status

The sidebar shows a drive icon indicating volume connectivity. Click it to see which volumes are currently mounted and their sync status.

Tip: For best redundancy, use at least two external volumes stored in different physical locations. LumiVault mirrors to all selected volumes during export.
