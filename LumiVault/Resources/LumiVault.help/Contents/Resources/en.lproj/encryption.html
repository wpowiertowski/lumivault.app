Encryption

LumiVault can encrypt your archived photos with AES-256-GCM, ensuring that files on external volumes and B2 are unreadable without your passphrase.

Setting Up Encryption

  1. Open Settings > Encryption (⌘,).
  2. Enter and confirm a passphrase.
  3. Click Create Key. LumiVault derives an encryption key using PBKDF2 with a random salt.

Encrypting Photos

During export, enable the Encryption toggle. Each file is encrypted individually:

  • Algorithm — AES-256-GCM (authenticated encryption).
  • Key derivation — PBKDF2 with your passphrase and a per-key salt.
  • Per-file nonce — each file gets a unique random nonce.
  • Authentication tag — GCM provides built-in integrity verification. Any tampering is detected during decryption.

Viewing Encrypted Photos

When you select an encrypted photo in the grid, LumiVault decrypts it on-the-fly for the preview. You must unlock the encryption key at app launch by entering your passphrase.

Encrypted File Format

Encrypted files use the .enc extension appended to the original filename (e.g., IMG_1234.jpg.enc). The nonce and authentication tag are stored within the encrypted file.

Important: If you lose your passphrase, encrypted files cannot be recovered. There is no recovery mechanism. Store your passphrase securely.
